Tetris

Tetris is a puzzle video game created by the Soviet
software engineer Alexey Pajitnov in 1985. It has been
published by several companies for multiple platforms,
most prominently during a dispute over the appropriation
of the rights in the late 1980s. After a significant period of
publication by Nintendo, the rights reverted to Pajitnov in 1996,
who co-founded the Tetris Company with Henk Rogers to manage licensing.


Portal

Portal consists primarily of a series of puzzles that
must be solved by teleporting the player's character
and simple objects using "the Aperture Science Handheld Portal Device",
often referred to as the "portal gun", a device that can create
inter-spatial portals between two flat planes.
